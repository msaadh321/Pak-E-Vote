How Your Vote is Secured

A step-by-step look at our encryption and verification process

1

Vote Creation

Your vote is encrypted on your device using your unique public key before it leaves your computer or mobile device.

2

Secure Transmission

Encrypted vote travels through secure TLS 1.3 channels to our distributed servers, protected from interception.

3

Zero-Knowledge Verification

System verifies vote validity using cryptographic proofs without decrypting or viewing your actual vote.

4

Blockchain Recording

Verified vote is recorded on immutable blockchain with timestamp and cryptographic hash for audit purposes.

5

Aggregate Counting

Votes are aggregated and counted in encrypted form, ensuring individual votes remain confidential.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about PakVote security

How is my vote kept secret?

Your vote is encrypted using AES-256 encryption before it leaves your device. The system uses zero-knowledge proofs to verify vote validity without decrypting it. Only the aggregate results are decrypted, ensuring individual votes remain confidential.

Can votes be tampered with?

No. Each vote is recorded on an immutable blockchain ledger. Any attempt to modify a vote would require changing every subsequent block in the chain, which is computationally impossible. The system also uses cryptographic signatures to detect tampering attempts.

What prevents multiple voting?

We use multiple layers of verification: CNIC validation, biometric authentication, and blockchain-based duplicate prevention. Once a vote is cast, your identity is cryptographically marked in the system to prevent additional votes.

Is the system vulnerable to hacking?

PakVote employs defense-in-depth security with multiple layers of protection. Regular penetration testing, bug bounty programs, and security audits by independent firms ensure vulnerabilities are identified and patched promptly. The system is designed to be resilient against common attack vectors.

How can I verify my vote was counted?

After voting, you receive a unique cryptographic receipt. This receipt allows you to verify that your vote was included in the final tally without revealing how you voted. The blockchain audit trail is publicly verifiable by independent observers.
